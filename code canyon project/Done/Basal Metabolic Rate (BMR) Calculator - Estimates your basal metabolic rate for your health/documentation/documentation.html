Introduction

The basal metabolic rate (BMR) is the amount of energy needed while resting in a temperate environment when the digestive system is inactive. It is the equivalent of figuring out how much gas an idle car consumes while parked. In such a state, energy will be used only to maintain vital organs, which include the heart, brain, kidneys, nervous system, intestines, liver, lungs, sex organs, muscles, and skin. For most people, upwards of ~70% of total energy (calories) burned each day is due to upkeep. Physical activity makes up ~20% of expenditure and ~10% is used for the digestion of food, also known as thermogenesis.

The BMR is measured under very restrictive circumstances while awake. An accurate BMR measurement requires that a person's sympathetic nervous system is inactive, which means the person must be completely rested. Basal metabolism is usually the largest component of a person's total caloric needs. The daily caloric need is the BMR value multiplied by a factor with a value between 1.2 and 1.9, depending on activity level.

The calculator is built using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and it also utilizes the popular JavaScript library jQuery.

Quick Start

Copy-paste the stylesheet link into your head before all other stylesheets to load our CSS.


<link href="css/Calc-style.css" rel="stylesheet" type="text/css">

Copy-paste the JavaScript & jQuery link into your body bottom all other after Html element.


<script src="js/jquery.js"></script>
<script src="js/Calc-script.js"></script>

Copy-paste the .ms-cl class into your body Element.

<html>
    <body>

        </div>

Features

Features
It allows you to choose between Metric and US units for your height and weight measurements.
It requires you to enter your height, weight, gender, age, and activity level to calculate your BMR.
It uses the Harris-Benedict formula to estimate your BMR at rest and at motion, based on your data and activity level.
It displays your BMR results in kcal per day, showing how many calories you burn when your body is at rest and when your body is in motion.

How To Use (HTML Structure)

The Basal Metabolic Rate Calculator's HTML structure consists of various sections and elements to handle different functionalities. The main sections include:

HTML Structure Description
.ms-cl The main container for the calculator.
.cl-wrapper The wrapper containing the input fields and output sections.
.err_msg A paragraph element to display an error message if not all required inputs are provided.
.check-box It appears to be part of the structure for user input related to unit selection and gender.
#calc_btn A button for triggering the BMR calculation.

Form Inputs

The following form inputs are available for the user to enter their Basal Metabolic Rate calculator details:

Inputs Description
Unit Selection - (.unit_check) This section is select unit - Metric Unit (kg), US Unit (lbs).
Gender Selection - (.gen_check) This section is select Gender - Male, Female.
Activity Level Selection - (#cl_activity) This section displaying Activity Level.

Additional Notes

  • Users can choose between Metric (kg) and US (lbs) units for entering height and weight.

  • Users can choose their activity level from a dropdown with four options, ranging from little or no exercise to hard daily exercise or sports and a physical job.
