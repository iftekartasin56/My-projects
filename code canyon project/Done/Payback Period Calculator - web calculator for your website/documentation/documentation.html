Introduction

Welcome to our Payback period calculator, The Payback period calculator to help determine the time required to recover your initial investment from your cash flow.

This calculator provides two main types of analysis

1.Steady cash flow - This category is ideal for projects or investments that generate consistent annual cash flows. By entering your discount rate, initial investment and annual cash flow, the calculator will provide the traditional payback period and the discounted payback period, which account for the time value of money.

2.Irregular Cash Flow Analysis: This category is suitable for investments with variable cash flows over time. You can input cash flows for up to five years. The calculator will then calculate the payback period based on the irregular cash flows.

The calculator is built using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript libray Jquery and Autonumeric number format.

Features

Features
Allows users to input a consistent annual cash flow amount for projects with steady returns.
Reflects the time value of money, adjusting the payback period to account for the discount rate.
Users can enter the discount rate as a percentage to reflect their cost of capital or desired rate of return.
The discount rate affects the discount payback period, making the analysis more comprehensive.
Users can input the total initial cost of the investment, which is used to calculate both the traditional and discounted payback periods.
Shows the number of years required to recover the initial investment without considering the discount rate.

Form Inputs

This HTML document appears to be a Payback period calculator, allowing users to input their Payback period.

Inputs Description
Discount rate - (#in_1) Enter the discount rate as a percentage to reflect the cost of capital or the desired rate of return.
Initial investment - (#in_2) Enter the total initial cost of the investment.
Annual cash flow - (#in_3) Enter the amount of adjusted annual cash flow generated by the investment.
Year 1 - (#in_4) Enter the cash flow amount for the first year.
Year 2 - (#in_5) Enter the cash flow amount for the second year.
Year 3 - (#in_6) Enter the cash flow amount for the third year.
Year 4 - (#in_7) Enter the cash flow amount for the fourth year.
Year 5 - (#in_8) Enter the cash flow amount for the fifth year.

Additional Notes

  • The payback period is the time it takes to recover the initial investment from the cash flows generated by the investment.

  • Traditional payback periods do not account for the time value of money, while discounted payback periods do.

  • For irregular cash flow analysis, input the exact cash flow amount for each year to get a precise calculation.

  • The calculated payback period and discounted payback period will be displayed as soon as the required inputs are entered.
