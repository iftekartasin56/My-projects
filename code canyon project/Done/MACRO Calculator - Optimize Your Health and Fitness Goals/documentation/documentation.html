Introduction

If you are looking for a way to optimize your diet and achieve your health and fitness goals, you might want to try using a macro calculator. A macro calculator is a tool that helps you determine the optimal ratio of macronutrients (protein, carbs, and fats) for your body type, activity level, and goal. Whether you want to maintain your current weight, lose weight, or gain weight, a macro calculator can help you customize your nutrition plan and track your progress.

This macro calculator is easy to use and user-friendly. All you need to do is enter some basic information about yourself, such as your age, gender, height, and weight. Then, you can select your goal and your activity level from the options provided. The macro calculator will then calculate your total calorie intake and the percentage of each macronutrient that you should consume. You can also adjust the sliders to customize your macro ratio according to your preferences. The macro calculator will also show you the amount of protein, carbs, and fats in grams that you need to eat per day.

Using this macro calculator, you can plan your meals and snacks accordingly and make sure that you are getting the right balance of nutrients for your body. You can also monitor your results and make changes as needed. A macro calculator is a great way to take control of your health and fitness and achieve your goals faster and easier.

The calculator is built using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and it also utilizes the popular JavaScript library jQuery and Autonumeric Number Format.

Form Inputs

The following form inputs are available for the user to enter their MACRO calculator details:

Inputs Description
Age - (#in_age) This section is input age.
Gender - (.in-gender) This section is select Gender - Male, Female.
Height - (.in-h-unit) This section displaying height (ft and m).
Weight - (.in-w-unit) This section displaying Weight (lbs and kg).
Goal - (.in-goal) Three options (Maintain Current Weight, Lose Weight, Gain Weight) with checkboxes.
Activity Level - (.in-act-level) Five options with checkboxes indicating the activity level.
Customize Your Macro Ratio - (.in-pr) Three inputs for protein, carbs, and fats percentages. An error message (#err-msg) is displayed if the total percentage does not equal 100.
