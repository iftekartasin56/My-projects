Introduction

Do you want to manage your money better and achieve your financial goals? If so, you might want to try the 50/30/20 budgeting method. This is a simple and effective way to allocate your monthly after-tax income into three categories: necessities, wants, and savings & debt repayment.

The 50/30/20 budget calculator can help you apply this method to your own situation. All you need to do is enter your monthly after-tax income and the calculator will automatically divide it into 50% for necessities, 30% for wants, and 20% for savings & debt repayment. You can also adjust these percentages according to your preferences and needs.

The calculator is built using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript and Jquery library and Autonumeric number format.

Quick Start

Copy-paste the stylesheet link into your head before all other stylesheets to load our CSS.


<link href="css/Calc-style.css" rel="stylesheet" type="text/css">

Copy-paste the JavaScript link into your body bottom all other after Html element.


<script src="js/Calc-script.js"></script>

Copy-paste the #hm_cl5 id into your body Element.

<html>
    <body>
        <div class="ms-cl">
            //write Your Html code
        </div>
    </body>
</html>

Features

Features
It helps you organize your monthly after-tax income into three categories: necessities, wants, and savings & debt repayment.
It follows the 50/30/20 rule, a popular budgeting method that splits your income according to your needs, wants, and goals.
It allows you to adjust the percentages of each category according to your preferences and needs.
It provides you with examples of expenses that fall under each category, such as food, shelter, transportation, insurance, shopping, entertainment, emergency fund, credit card debts, and more.

Additional Notes

  • The 50/30/20 rule is not a one-size-fits-all solution. You can adjust the percentages according to your personal circumstances and goals. For example, if you have a lot of debt, you might want to allocate more than 20% of your income to savings and debt repayment.

  • The 50/30/20 rule is a guideline, not a strict rule. You donâ€™t have to follow it exactly every month. Some months, you might spend more on wants or less on savings, depending on your situation.

  • The 50/30/20 rule can help you achieve financial stability and security. By spending 50% of your income on necessities, you can cover your basic needs and avoid overspending. By spending 30% of your income on wants, you can enjoy your life and reward yourself. By saving or paying off debt with 20% of your income, you can build your wealth and prepare for the future.
